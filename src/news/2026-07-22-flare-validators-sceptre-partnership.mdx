## what we run

Flare validation is more than block production. A validator only earns — for itself and its
delegators — if its entity also submits accurate FTSO price feeds every voting round and
serves Flare Data Connector attestations. We operate the full stack:

- **4 validators** on dedicated bare-metal in our own racks (AS142108)
- **FTSO data provider** — 63 price feeds, anchor + fast-updates, sourced in part from
  on-chain DEX data via our own chain indexers
- **FDC attestation** — verifier infrastructure for BTC, DOGE, XRP, ETH and Songbird
- Full monitoring: reward eligibility, feed accuracy, uptime, release tracking

## the partnership

[Sceptre](https://sceptre.fi) runs sFLR, the largest liquid-staking token on Flare. Through
this partnership, Sceptre's stake secures protocol-aligned validators instead of paying fees
to unaffiliated third-party operators — the validator-layer rewards flow back to the protocol.
Holder yield is unchanged; the protocol underneath it gets stronger every epoch.

660M FLR is staked to our validators today: two nodes at the 300M cap each, plus our two
original validators. All four pass every reward condition (staking, FTSO anchor feeds,
fast-updates, FDC) with zero strikes.

The structure also carries a yield edge no third-party arrangement can offer: a validator's
self-bond pays no delegation fee — it earns the **full** staking reward, where delegated stake
keeps 80%. Because the protocol funds the self-bond of these validators, those fee-free
rewards accrue back to sFLR instead of to an outside operator. Per FLR, the self-bonded slice
earns 25% more than the same FLR would as a plain delegation, and the edge grows with every
node added.

## stable rewards by design

On Flare, staking rewards are not unconditional. Under the FSP incentive rules (FIP.10), an
entity that fails any protocol requirement — FTSO anchor accuracy, fast-updates volume, FDC
attestation rate, validator uptime — loses passes, and with no passes left a single bad epoch
**burns the rewards across every stream** for that epoch. Delegating to a validator means
inheriting the operational quality of whoever runs its FTSO and FDC pipeline.

That is the point of this partnership: the validators, the price feeds, and the attestation
infrastructure are operated as one monitored system — reward eligibility, feed accuracy, and
uptime are alerted on around the clock, and client versions are tracked against upstream
releases automatically. The result is what a staking protocol actually needs from its
validator layer: not the occasional lucky epoch, but **predictable, uninterrupted reward
eligibility, every epoch**.

## why it matters for Flare

Every independent, high-uptime validator with its own FTSO pipeline makes the network's price
oracle harder to manipulate and its consensus more decentralized. We source feeds from our own
indexers rather than repeating aggregator APIs, which adds genuine signal diversity to the
median.
